IELTS Writing Five Distinct Essay Types

In task 2, you are required to write an essay of at least 250 words.

1. Opinion (Agree or Disagree) Essay

You are given a statement and asked to what extent you agree or disagree, requiring you to take a clear stance and defend it.

Requirements:

A clear stance. You must state whether you agree, disagree, or partially agree, and spend the essay defending that specific position.

Structure:

Use this structure when you must defend a single, clear position.
  • Introduction:
    • Paraphrase the prompt.
    • Thesis Statement: Clearly state whether you agree, disagree, or take a balanced view.
  • Body Paragraph 1: First reason supporting your thesis (Topic sentence => Explanation => Example).
  • Body Paragraph 2: Second reason supporting your thesis (Topic sentence => Explanation => Example).
  • Conclusion: Restate your thesis in different words and briefly summarize your main points.

Prompt:

“Some people believe that artificial intelligence will eventually replace human teachers in the classroom. To what extent do you agree or disagree?”

Sample Essay:

INTRODUCTION
[PARAPHRASE: Some individuals argue that artificial intelligence will ultimately substitute human educators in educational institutions.] [THESIS: I completely disagree with this view because AI lacks the emotional intelligence necessary for teaching and cannot deliver personalized mentorship.]
BODY PARAGRAPH 1
[TOPIC SENTENCE: Firstly, human teachers possess essential interpersonal skills that machines simply cannot replicate.] [EXPLANATION: Education is not merely the transmission of raw facts; it requires empathy, motivation, and emotional support.] [EXAMPLE: For instance, an experienced teacher can notice when a student is feeling anxious or discouraged and adapt their approach to offer comfort, whereas an algorithm only processes data points without understanding human emotion.]
BODY PARAGRAPH 2
[TOPIC SENTENCE: Secondly, educators serve as vital role models who guide moral and character development.] [EXPLANATION: Classroom learning involves instilling values such as discipline, cooperation, and ethics, which require authentic human interaction.] [EXAMPLE: A computer program can deliver a lecture on fairness, but only a human instructor can model ethical decision-making during classroom conflicts or mentor students through personal struggles.]
CONCLUSION
[RESTATE THESIS: In conclusion, I strongly reject the idea that artificial intelligence could ever fully replace educators.] [SUMMARY: Although AI tools can assist with administrative tasks and content delivery, the essential human elements of empathy and moral mentorship ensure that real teachers remain indispensable in the classroom.]
Word count: 257 words

2. Discussion Essay

You must objectively explain both sides of a debate before offering your own opinion.

Requirements:

A balanced argument/structure. You must devote one body paragraph to explaining the logic behind longer prison sentences, another to explaining the logic behind rehabilitation, and clearly provide your own opinion.

Structure:

Use this when you must examine two opposing sides and give your own view.
  • Introduction:
    • Paraphrase the prompt (mentioning both sides of the debate).
    • Thesis Statement: State which of the two sides you support.
  • Body Paragraph 1: Discuss the side you disagree with (explain why people hold this view, even if you do not).
  • Body Paragraph 2: Discuss the side you agree with (explain why this view is stronger and justify your opinion).
  • Conclusion: Summarize both viewpoints and re-emphasize your final opinion.

Prompt:

“Some people think that the best way to reduce crime rates is through longer prison sentences. Others argue that education and rehabilitation are more effective. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.”

Sample Essay:

INTRODUCTION
[PARAPHRASE: While some people argue that extending jail terms is the most effective approach to lowering crime rates, others contend that educational programs and rehabilitation yield better results.] [THESIS: Although prolonged imprisonment offers immediate punishment, I believe rehabilitation is a vastly superior long-term solution.]
BODY PARAGRAPH 1
[DISAGREED VIEW: Advocates of longer prison sentences argue that severe penalties serve as a powerful deterrent to potential criminals.] [EXPLANATION: The logic is that when consequences are harsh, individuals will think twice before breaking the law, thereby keeping dangerous offenders off the streets for extended periods.] [EXAMPLE: For example, mandatory minimum sentences for serious violent offenses can temporarily lower local crime statistics by isolating repeat offenders from society.]
BODY PARAGRAPH 2
[AGREED VIEW: On the other hand, proponents of rehabilitation emphasize that addressing the root causes of criminal behavior is far more constructive.] [EXPLANATION: Providing inmates with job skills and counseling reduces recidivism rates, as former convicts can reintegrate into society as productive citizens rather than returning to a life of crime.] [EXAMPLE: Countries like Norway, which prioritize prisoner education over harsh sentences, maintain some of the lowest re-offending rates in the world.]
CONCLUSION
[SUMMARY & FINAL OPINION: In conclusion, while lengthy incarceration briefly removes criminals from public life, rehabilitation tackles the underlying social issues.] [RE-EMPHASIZE OPINION: Therefore, I firmly support educational programs as the best long-term strategy for crime reduction.]
Word count: 252 words

3. Advantages and Disadvantages Essay

You evaluate the pros and cons of a specific trend or situation.

Requirements:

Objective analysis. One body paragraph should outline the main benefits (e.g., cost savings, flexibility), while the next outlines the main drawbacks (e.g., lack of personal connection, technical issues). (Note: If the prompt asks "Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?", it turns into an Opinion essay where you must choose a side).

Structure:

Use this when evaluating pros and cons. If asked "Do advantages outweigh disadvantages?", use the Opinion structure instead.
  • Introduction:
    • Paraphrase the prompt (state that the trend has both positive and negative aspects).
    • Outline Statement: State that you will examine both the benefits and drawbacks.
  • Body Paragraph 1: Focus entirely on the Advantages (State 1-2 benefits => Explain => Example).
  • Body Paragraph 2: Focus entirely on the Disadvantages (State 1-2 drawbacks => Explain => Example).
  • Conclusion: Summarize the main advantages and disadvantages in a single sentence.

Prompt:

“More and more people are doing business and conducting meetings through online video calls. What are the advantages and disadvantages of this trend?”

Sample Essay:

INTRODUCTION
[PARAPHRASE: An increasing number of individuals and organizations are relying on video conferencing platforms to conduct business meetings.] [OUTLINE STATEMENT: This essay will examine both the benefits and drawbacks of this growing reliance on remote communication tools.]
BODY PARAGRAPH 1
[ADVANTAGES: The primary advantage of virtual meetings is the significant saving of time and financial resources.] [EXPLANATION: By conducting business online, companies eliminate the need for expensive corporate travel and lengthy commutes, allowing employees to spend more time on productive work.] [EXAMPLE: For instance, multinational corporations can hold international strategy sessions instantly without paying for international flights, hotel accommodations, or physical venue rentals.]
BODY PARAGRAPH 2
[DISADVANTAGES: Conversely, a major disadvantage is the lack of genuine personal connection and potential technical difficulties.] [EXPLANATION: Digital interactions often lack subtle body language, making trust-building and negotiation more challenging, while unstable internet connections can interrupt critical discussions.] [EXAMPLE: Important deals may falter or face miscommunication because participants fail to pick up on non-verbal cues over a lagging video feed.]
CONCLUSION
[SUMMARY: In conclusion, doing business via video calls offers major advantages in convenience and cost efficiency, but it also carries distinct disadvantages regarding interpersonal connection and technical reliability.]
Word count: 255 words

4. Problem and Solution (or Cause and Solution) Essay

This is an explanatory essay rather than an argumentative one. You identify why a specific problem exists and propose realistic fixes for it.

Requirements:

Cause-and-effect thinking. You must use the body paragraphs to outline the primary causes (e.g., increased screen time, sedentary school curricula) and then offer practical, realistic solutions to those specific problems.

Structure:

Use this to explain causes/effects and offer resolutions.
  • Introduction:
  • Paraphrase the prompt (acknowledge the existence of the issue).
  • Outline Statement: State that you will discuss the causes (or problems) and offer solutions.
  • Body Paragraph 1 (Problems/Causes): Explain 1-2 main causes or problems (Topic sentence => Explanation => Example).
  • Body Paragraph 2 (Solutions): Provide direct solutions to the exact problems mentioned in Body Paragraph 1.
  • Conclusion: Summarize the key problem(s) and restate the most effective solution.

    • Prompt:

    “In many countries, young people are becoming less interested in physical exercise. Why is this happening, and what can be done to encourage them?”

    Sample Essay:

    INTRODUCTION
    [PARAPHRASE: In numerous nations, youth participation in physical activities and sports is noticeably declining.] [OUTLINE STATEMENT: This essay will discuss the main causes behind this trend and propose practical solutions to address the issue.]
    BODY PARAGRAPH 1
    [PROBLEMS/CAUSES: The primary reason for this growing disinterest is the rise of screen-based entertainment combined with sedentary lifestyles.] [EXPLANATION: Today's youth spend hours engaging with smartphones, video games, and social media, which offer instant gratification without physical effort.] [EXAMPLE: Consequently, many teenagers prefer playing virtual sports on gaming consoles after school rather than joining real local sports teams or exercising outdoors.]
    BODY PARAGRAPH 2
    [SOLUTIONS: To solve this problem, governments and educational institutions must collaborate to make physical activity more accessible and appealing.] [EXPLANATION: Schools should modernize their physical education curricula by introducing diverse, engaging options beyond traditional competitive sports, while parents should set strict boundaries on screen time.] [EXAMPLE: For instance, offering activities like modern dance, rock climbing, or martial arts in schools can spark interest in students who dislike standard team sports.]
    CONCLUSION
    [SUMMARY: In conclusion, excessive digital entertainment is the main cause of physical inactivity among young people.] [RESTATE SOLUTION: However, this issue can be solved by modernizing school exercise programs and actively limiting daily screen time.]
    Word count: 254 words

    5. Two-Part/Direct Question Essay

    You are asked two distinct, sometimes unrelated questions about a topic (e.g., "Why is this happening? Is it a positive development?"). You must simply answer both parts directly.

    Requirements:

    Direct answers. You must dedicate Body Paragraph 1 to answering the first question (the "why") and Body Paragraph 2 to answering the second question (your evaluation of the trend).

    Structure:

    Use this when the prompt asks two distinct, explicit questions.
    • Introduction:
      • Paraphrase the prompt.
      • Thesis Statement: Directly answer both Question 1 and Question 2 in brief.
    • Body Paragraph 1: Answer Question 1 (Topic sentence/direct answer => Explanation => Example).
    • Body Paragraph 2: Answer Question 2 (Topic sentence/direct answer => Explanation => Example).
    • Conclusion: Summarize your answers to both questions.

    Prompt:

    “It is becoming very common these days for students to take courses over the internet instead of in face-to-face classrooms. Why are more students choosing this way of learning? Is this a positive or a negative development?”

    Sample Essay:

    INTRODUCTION
    [PARAPHRASE: Nowadays, an increasing number of students opt for online learning rather than traditional classroom instruction.] [THESIS: Students choose this method primarily for its flexibility, and I view this as a overall positive development because it democratizes access to higher education.]
    BODY PARAGRAPH 1
    [ANSWER QUESTION 1: The main reason students choose online courses is the unmatched flexibility and convenience they offer.] [EXPLANATION: Web-based programs allow learners to study at their own pace and schedule classes around work or family commitments.] [EXAMPLE: For example, working professionals can complete degree programs at night without sacrificing their full-time jobs or commuting to a physical campus.]
    BODY PARAGRAPH 2
    [ANSWER QUESTION 2: I believe this trend is a positive development because it vastly expands educational opportunities globally.] [EXPLANATION: Digital learning breaks down geographical and financial barriers, enabling individuals from remote areas or lower-income backgrounds to access top-tier university curricula at reduced costs.] [EXAMPLE: A student in a developing country can now earn a certificate from a world-renowned university without the immense cost of moving abroad.]
    CONCLUSION
    [SUMMARY: In conclusion, students favor online education due to its convenience and flexibility, and this is a positive trend that successfully makes quality education accessible to a wider global audience.]
    Word count: 251 words
